# Persona 1 — “Time-Poor Beginner Gamer” (Sprint 1)

## Persona Summary
**Name:** Mina  
**Age:** 20  
**Occupation:** University student  
**Gaming Profile:** Beginner / casual player, plays in short sessions (1–2 hours), prefers clear guidance over long videos  
**Devices/Platform:** Laptop / Console  
**Goal:** Progress efficiently without feeling overwhelmed or wasting time

## Background
Mina recently started playing popular games recommended by friends. She enjoys gaming but often struggles with game mechanics, mission objectives, and decision-making under pressure. She is motivated to improve but has limited time due to university responsibilities.

## Goals
- Find **fast, practical solutions** to specific game challenges (e.g., boss fights, beginner builds).
- Learn the game fundamentals without reading long guides.
- Improve confidence and reduce frustration.
- Discover tips created by trustworthy community members.

## Pain Points
- Too many sources online, difficult to know what is correct.
- Advice is often aimed at experienced players, not beginners.
- Video content can be time-consuming when she only needs one answer.
- Some communities are unwelcoming or toxic towards beginners.

## Motivations
- Wants a supportive environment where asking questions is normal.
- Prefers content that is organised by game and topic.
- Values step-by-step explanations and helpful examples.

## Typical Scenario (User Journey)
1. Mina is stuck on a mission objective and wants a quick solution.
2. She visits the platform and browses the **Tips Listing** for the relevant game.
3. She opens a **Tip Detail** page and follows the recommended steps.
4. She checks the tip author’s **User Profile** to find more beginner-friendly tips.
5. She continues playing with improved confidence.

## Opportunities for Design
- Clear navigation by game and topic tags.
- “Beginner-friendly” labels and difficulty indicators.
- A respectful community tone with moderation policies.
- Short, structured tips: “Problem → Steps → Expected result”.
